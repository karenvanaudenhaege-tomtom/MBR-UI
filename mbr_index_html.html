Note — Highway-only product (ongoing)
▶
Decision window remains open through end of April. Volvo does not require a dedicated highway-only product today; CARIAD quality and safety requirements still being assessed.

There was previously an HDT on whether to continue, pause, or stop development of the highway-only map product. Volvo can filter non-highway data themselves and no other OEM has confirmed requirements. Infrastructure and maintenance costs remain high.

However, CARIAD may have specific quality and safety requirements related to a highway-only map. To avoid stopping prematurely, development continues until end of April, allowing the Fermi team to complete the batch component and establish the technical foundation before an informed go/no-go decision.

Risk 1 — Violation Store readiness & CC downstream integration
▶
OPS violation resolution is at increasing risk as scope expands beyond lanes to routing features (speeds, one-ways, control access). In the current model, OPS can only start resolving violations after product completion, leaving very limited time before customer delivery. Not feasible at current and expected volumes.

Upstream adoption of CC rules across value streams is currently weak and has known feature gaps (e.g., teams needing a "bound state of multiple layers" to perform CC rules upstream). Downstream OPS continues to absorb violations across value streams exceeding available resources.

Clustering improves throughput by bundling violations into a single task, but clusters mix severities (critical, warnings, false positives) and the system treats all tasks as equal. Severity information exists in RMS but is not available in CC run outputs used operationally. Outside of Toyota, there is no consistent "master rule list" with severity tagging.

This creates a prioritization risk (OPS cannot focus on highest-impact items) and a reporting/traceability risk, especially for Toyota quality reporting. Violation Store is expected to mitigate this by preserving violation history and enabling false-positive stamping.

Recommendation: Treat as a delivery and customer-commitment risk (Toyota in particular). Prioritize: (1) CC downstream → Violation Store ingestion with false-positive stamping, (2) severity-based prioritization using CC team severity list, (3) find owner for severity metadata exposure, (4) structured plan to increase upstream CC adoption.

Risk 2 — Unifying customer feedback for LM product
▶
Lane Model feedback reporting is fragmented across multiple channels. Current IM framework not aligned with LM maturity level — most issues are structural, requiring cross-functional collaboration. Detection mechanisms not yet fully established (e.g., Control Checks only available for Basemap today).

ADAS team is seeking one unified way of receiving all possible feedback so that Lane Model issues can trigger the right actions and guide downstream processes effectively.

F&R has created a proposal to extend the unified E2E feedback workflow (created for road model) to lane model feedback with a customized flow suitable for handling the volume, triage, resolution and reporting for the ADAS team.

The solution extends the OrbIT ecosystem (Map Feedback API, Issue Store, Task Manager, Map Feedback Hub) and introduces new components like Violation Store and Triage for structured intake, classification, prioritization and routing of LM feedback.

Integration of Cancun Lane Model feedback into Issue Store is in progress to position OrbIT as the first-class ecosystem for LM feedback within a unified flow.
